cats

1-Domestication and History:

Cats (Felis catus) are domesticated carnivorous mammals that belong to the Felidae family.
They are believed to have been domesticated around 9,000 years ago in the Near East,
where they played a crucial role in controlling rodent populations around early human settlements.
Over time, cats spread to various parts of the world, adapting to different environments and becoming valued companions.

2-Physical Characteristics:

Cats come in a wide variety of breeds, each with its own unique characteristics.
They range in size from small breeds like the Singapura to larger breeds like the Maine Coon.
Cats are known for their retractable claws, sharp senses (especially keen night vision),
and a flexible body that allows them to land on their feet after a fall, a behavior known as the "righting reflex."
